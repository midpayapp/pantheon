DETAILED WALKTHROUGH

Let's take a closer look at how MidPay works for your construction business.

Step 1: Create Project Agreement

When you secure a new construction project, you'll create a project agreement in MidPay. This takes just a few minutes and can be done from any device.

What You'll Define:

  • • Project Details: Name, location, timeline, and description
  • • Milestones: Break down the project into specific phases (e.g., foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, finishing)
  • • Payment Schedule: Assign dollar amounts to each milestone
  • • Verification Requirements: Specify what constitutes completion (photos, inspections, etc.)

Once complete, the agreement is sent to your client for review and approval. They can suggest changes or accept as is.

Step 2: Client Funds Escrow

Before work begins, your client deposits the project funds into MidPay's secure escrow account. This is a critical step that ensures you'll be paid for your work.

How It Works:

  • • Client Receives Link: After approving the agreement, your client receives a secure payment link
  • • Secure Deposit: They can fund via ACH transfer, credit card, or wire transfer
  • • FDIC-Insured: Funds are held in FDIC-insured accounts for maximum security
  • • Transparent Status: Both you and your client can see that funds are secured

Once escrow is funded, you'll receive a notification that it's safe to begin work. No more wondering if the money is there!

Step 3: Complete & Get Paid

As you complete each milestone in your project, you'll submit verification through the MidPay platform. Once verified, payment is released immediately.

The Verification Process:

  • • Submit Completion: Upload photos, videos, or inspection documents showing completed work
  • • Client Review: Client receives notification to review and approve the milestone
  • • Payment Release: Upon approval, funds are released from escrow to your account within 24 hours
  • • Milestone Communication: We call homeowners to verify satisfaction at each milestone

This process repeats for each milestone until the project is complete. You maintain healthy cash flow throughout the project instead of waiting for one final payment.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common questions about how MidPay works.

How long does it take to receive payment after milestone approval?

Once a milestone is approved by the homeowner, funds are released from escrow and deposited into your account within 24 hours. This is significantly faster than the industry average of 45+ days for construction payments.

What happens if a client disputes a milestone?

If a client has concerns about a milestone, our Proactive Milestone Communication team will facilitate a conversation between both parties to address the issues immediately. Our dedicated team contacts homeowners directly to verify satisfaction with each completed milestone, preventing concerns from accumulating until the end of the project. This personal approach ensures that any issues are identified and resolved promptly, keeping projects moving forward smoothly.

Can I use MidPay for all my construction projects?

Yes! MidPay is designed to work for all types of construction projects, from small renovations to large new builds. The platform is flexible enough to accommodate different project structures, timelines, and payment schedules. You can use it for as many projects as you like, with no limit.

How do I convince clients to use MidPay?

Many contractors find that offering MidPay as a payment option actually helps them win more business. Homeowners appreciate the transparency, security, and milestone-based approach. We provide marketing materials that explain the benefits to homeowners, including protection from incomplete work and clear expectations for what they're paying for at each stage.

What if I need to make changes to the project after it's started?

MidPay makes it easy to handle change orders. You can add new milestones or modify existing ones with client approval. When a change order is approved, the client will fund any additional amount to the escrow account, ensuring you're covered for the extra work.
