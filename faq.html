What is MidPay?

MidPay is a secure payment platform specifically designed for the construction industry. We use escrow protection to ensure contractors get paid for completed work and homeowners only pay for quality results. Our milestone-based payment system allows funds to be released incrementally as work is completed and verified, providing security and transparency for all parties involved.

How does the escrow process work?

The MidPay escrow process works in these steps:

  1. The contractor creates a project agreement with defined milestones and payment amounts.
  2. The homeowner reviews and approves the agreement.
  3. The homeowner funds the escrow account via ACH transfer, credit card, or wire transfer.
  4. Funds are held in a secure, FDIC-insured escrow account.
  5. As the contractor completes each milestone, they submit verification (photos, videos, inspection documents).
  6. The homeowner reviews the completed work and approves the milestone.
  7. Upon approval, funds for that milestone are released from escrow to the contractor within 24 hours.
  8. This process repeats for each milestone until the project is complete.

This structured approach ensures that funds are secure, work is verified, and payments are released promptly for completed milestones.

What payment methods can be used to fund escrow?

Homeowners can fund their escrow accounts using several payment methods:

  • ACH Bank Transfer: Direct transfer from a bank account (no fee)
  • Credit/Debit Card: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover (3% processing fee)
  • Wire Transfer: For larger projects or international payments (varies by bank)

For larger projects, we recommend ACH or wire transfers to minimize processing fees. The platform will guide you through the funding process and provide clear information about any applicable fees.

How are milestone payments structured?

Milestone payments are structured based on the specific phases of your construction project. The contractor will define these milestones in the project agreement, typically following industry-standard project phases.

For example, a kitchen remodel might have these milestones:

  1. Demolition and preparation (15% of total)
  2. Rough plumbing and electrical (20% of total)
  3. Cabinets and countertops (30% of total)
  4. Appliance installation (20% of total)
  5. Final touches and cleanup (15% of total)

Each milestone has a specific dollar amount assigned to it, and funds are released incrementally as each milestone is completed and verified. This approach ensures that payment is aligned with project progress.

What happens to the money while it's in escrow?

While your funds are in escrow, they are held in FDIC-insured accounts separate from MidPay's operating funds. The money remains secure and untouched until milestone completion is verified and approved.

Escrow funds do not earn interest, as they are held in non-interest-bearing accounts to comply with escrow regulations. This ensures that the exact amount funded is available for release to the contractor upon milestone completion.

You can view the status of your escrow account at any time through the MidPay platform, seeing exactly how much has been funded, released, and remains in escrow.

Can I get a refund if my project is cancelled?

Yes, if a project is cancelled before all milestones are completed, any remaining funds in escrow will be returned to the homeowner. The refund process is as follows:

  1. Either party can initiate a project cancellation through the platform.
  2. Both parties must agree to the cancellation terms, including which milestones have been completed and which remain unfinished.
  3. Funds for completed and approved milestones remain with the contractor.
  4. Funds for incomplete milestones are returned to the homeowner within 5 business days.
  5. If there's a dispute about cancellation terms, our dispute resolution process will help reach a fair solution.

This process ensures that contractors are paid for work they've completed while homeowners receive refunds for work that hasn't been done.

How do contractors receive their payments?

Contractors receive payments through their preferred payout method, which they set up when creating their MidPay account. Available payout options include:

  • Direct Deposit (ACH): Funds transferred directly to the contractor's bank account (1-2 business days)
  • Instant Deposit: Immediate transfer to a linked debit card (1% fee, $10 maximum)
  • MidPay Balance: Funds held in the contractor's MidPay account for future use or later withdrawal

Once a milestone is approved, the funds are released from escrow and processed according to the contractor's chosen payout method. Standard transfers typically take 1-2 business days to appear in the contractor's bank account.

If a homeowner is unresponsive after multiple contact attempts, our team will conduct a review of the submitted documentation and may release payment after a reasonable waiting period, provided the work appears to meet the specifications outlined in the contract.

Can I switch between pricing plans?

Yes, contractors can switch between the Premium Plan and Free Plan at any time through their account settings. When switching plans:

  • Free to Premium: The change takes effect immediately, and you'll be billed the monthly fee right away. You'll immediately gain access to all Premium features, and the 2% escrow fee will apply to all new projects.
  • Premium to Free: The change takes effect at the end of your current billing cycle. You'll maintain Premium features until that date, after which you'll move to the Free Plan with the 5% escrow fee for new projects.

Existing projects will maintain the escrow fee rate that was in effect when the project was created, regardless of plan changes. This ensures consistency throughout the project lifecycle.

How is billing handled for contractors?

Billing for contractors depends on your chosen plan:

  • Premium Plan ($199/month): You'll be billed monthly on the anniversary of your sign-up date. Payment is automatically charged to your preferred payment method (credit card or ACH).
  • Free Plan (no monthly fee): There's no monthly billing. The 5% escrow fee is automatically deducted from each milestone payment as it's released from escrow.

For both plans, you'll receive detailed invoices and payment receipts via email and in your account dashboard. You can view your billing history, update payment methods, and download statements for accounting purposes at any time.

How do I cancel my MidPay account?

You can cancel your MidPay account at any time through your account settings. The cancellation process varies slightly depending on your user type:

For Contractors:

  1. Complete all active projects or transfer them to another payment method.
  2. Go to Account Settings and select "Cancel Account."
  3. Select your reason for cancellation (this helps us improve our service).
  4. Confirm cancellation.

If you're on the Premium Plan, cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing cycle. Any escrow fees for completed milestones are non-refundable.

For Homeowners:

  1. Ensure all projects are completed or cancelled, with appropriate fund disbursement.
  2. Go to Account Settings and select "Cancel Account."
  3. Select your reason for cancellation.
  4. Confirm cancellation.

Account data is retained for a period of time in accordance with our data retention policy, but personal information is anonymized upon cancellation.
